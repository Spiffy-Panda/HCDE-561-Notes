# Session 1 — Three-C Reflection

## Curiosity

How do you go beyond an open-ended prompt for design work? General chat interfaces like Claude are incredibly powerful, but the AIs embedded in single-use design products feel frozen by comparison. The gap might not be about raw model capability — it could be about who controls the context window. A dedicated tool with a weaker model but tight context (your design system, your component library, your brand) might actually outperform a general interface with an open-ended prompt.

Related: Jenny Wen (Head of Design for Claude, ex-Figma) gave a talk at Hatch Conference 2025 called "Don't Trust the Process" arguing the traditional design process has become too rigid for today's tools. Time spent mocking and prototyping has dropped from 60-70% to 30-40%, replaced by direct pairing with engineers. This validates the observation that the embedded-AI-vs-general-interface question is live in the profession right now.

## Connections

As an HCII specialist, VR and AI are both powerful interfaces — both are "presence" technologies in different senses. VR creates spatial presence; a good AI interface creates something like cognitive presence, the feeling that it understands your situation. That framing is more generative for design work than a raw capability comparison.

Coming from a technical background, the real work is learning to think like a designer — translating technical instincts into design questions. Open-ended prompting is already a familiar skill; the gap is knowing what to ask about users. The VR background is useful here: thinking about presence and embodiment from a user perspective is latent design intuition, even without the formal design language.

## Creating Value

Reverse-engineering what good design feels like before being able to articulate why. Concrete examples from prior work:

- **Persona/questionnaire generator with LLM simulation** — Built this tool because no single-use product handled the workflow well enough. Needed Claude-level flexibility embedded in a structured process.
- **ForageMap AI persona work** — Used Claude for persona generation and it outperformed a teammate's 6.5-hour manual process. A real data point about where general interfaces beat specialized tools for design tasks.
- **YouTube epilepsy detection post** — Identified a real user need, knew the technical solution existed, and asked why the product gap persisted. Design thinking, even without calling it that at the time.

The through-line: translating between two fluencies — technical/AI and design/user-centered. That's the position this HCAI certificate is built for.
